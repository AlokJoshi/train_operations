How to play

Click on the Start Button to begin the game. The game will run for time units. Each time unit is shown at the center of the screen and will take about minutes of playing time. The time for which the entire game is paused is not counted.

Your objective to earn as much profit as possible by running up to a total of 9 trains. You start with a cash in hand of million dollars and 2 passenger trains and 1 freight train.

You can pause the entire game at any time by pressing the P key on the keyboard. P also starts the game if it is paused. When the game is in the paused state, the time units will not progress but you will still be able to add trains, stations and flyovers.

The T key displays the trains dialog box. It also allows you to monitor trains and start a new Train. Pressing the T key again removes the Train dialog box. The passenger trains can have a maximum of coaches and the freight trains can have a maximum of freight wagons. Each passenger coach costs and each freight wagon costs . You can add or remove coaches or freight wagons by using the + and - buttons in the train dialog box. You can also stop or pause any train by using the pause button in the train dialog box or by pressing the corresponding number key for each train (1 for Train #1, 2 for Train #2 and so on). There is an option to delete a train completely but if you do that, you will lose all the money you invested in that train and you will not be able to get it back. When a new train is started, it comes with a standard engine which costs . However, you can upgrade the engine of freight trains later in the game to increase the speed of the freight trains by clicking on the upgrade button in the train dialog box. Each engine upgrade costs . There is also an option to extend the train route by clicking on the extend button in the train dialog box. This extension can be done from either end of the train track. After clicking on the extend button, you will need to click on the map to specify the additional route for the train. Once the additional route is specified, you can either confirm the extension by clicking on the check button in the train dialog box or cancel the extension by clicking on the times button in the train dialog box. Below the train level controls, there is a button to start the track specification for a new train. After clicking on that button, you will need to click on the map to specify the route for the new train and then click on the flag off button in the train dialog box to start the new train. You can also cancel the track specification for the new train by clicking on the cancel button in the train dialog box. You will need to specify the type of the train (passenger or freight), the number of coaches or freight wagons and the speed of the train before flagging off the new train.

Similarly S brings up the Stations dialog box to add more stations to any train's route. Each station costs . However, adding a station allows you to earn more revenue from passengers or freight that is dropped off at that station or picked up from that station.

Similarly F brings up the Flyover dialog box to add a flyover at any point where two or more trains cross each other and where they are likely to collide. Note that flyovers are expensive each costs . Hence if you do not want to add them, you can manually stop one train to avoid the collision as described above. Each collision costs .

Before starting a new train, you may want to know where the cities and passengers are. You can see this information by clicking on the X key.

The raw material availablility is shown on the map if you click on the Y key.

The raw material demand is shown on the map if you click on the Z key.

It is assumed that passengers are moving to and from the cities and hence the passenger demand is higher between cities with higher populations. If the train stops at a station it can pick up passengers or freight and some neighboring passengers or freight may also be available to pick up. The more stations a train stops at, the more passengers or freight it can pick up and drop off and hence the more revenue it can earn. However, stopping at more stations takes time and also increases the cost since facilities need to be provided at each station. Hence you need to find the right balance to maximize your profit. If passengers or freight cannot be picked up at a station because the train is full, it will be shown as (x??) in the station dialog box.

Pressing the R key brings up the Results dialog box which displays the revenue, expenses and profit for each train and overall. You can use this information to make informed decisions about how to manage your trains, stations and flyovers to maximize your profit.

Please note that you can only add trains, stations, and flyovers if you have enough funds.

If you press the ? key, the game will display the 'How to play' dialog box.

Keep in mind the following obvious information:

  • Passenger trains earn you money based on the number of passengers they carry. Each passenger coach you add costs money. You pay for the maintenance cost of the track at the end of each period. based on the length of the track and stations on the route also cost money. Each coach has a capacity of passengers.
  • Freight trains earn you money based on the amount of freight they carry from the source to the destination. They also cost you money based on the number of stations they stop at.
  • Adding a flyover costs you 20 million dollars but it allows you to avoid collisions which can cause significant damage to your trains and hence significant loss of money because the two trains involved in the collision are out of commission for a certain period of time. In addition to this, you have to compensate for the loss of life and property.
  • Adding a station costs you 1 million dollars but it allows you to earn more revenue from passengers or freight. Also if there is a station at the intersection of two or more trains, they can cross each other without the need for a flyover and hence you can save money by not building a flyover.
